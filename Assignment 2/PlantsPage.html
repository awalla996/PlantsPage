Monstera Plant ~

The Monstera Plant nicknamed the Swiss Cheese plant. Can you guess why? It's because of all the holes in the plant. This plant is a tropical plant descending from the Araceae family. These plants typically grow to be enoromous in tropical jungles. This plant can be mistaken for split-leaf philodendron (they're apart of the same family). Monstera needs lots of space, so if you are planning on getting one make sure you have the space to accomdate it. There should be a balance between sun and shade. Too much sun results in yellow leaves and too much shade results in leaves that will grow toward dark areas (avoiding light).


Aloe Vera Plant ~

Aloe vera is a succulent plant species of the genus Aloe. The plant is stemless or very short-stemmed with thick, greenish, fleshy leaves that fan out from the plant’s central stem. The margin of the leaf is serrated with small teeth. Before you buy an aloe, note that you’ll need a location that offers bright, indirect sunlight (or artificial sunlight). Direct sunlight can dry out the plant too much and turn its fleshy leaves yellow, so you may need to water more often if your aloe lives in an especially sunny spot. Place in bright, indirect sunlight or artificial light. Aloe vera do best in temperatures between 55 and 80°F.


Snake Plant ~

There are no stems on the snake plant, just tough, thick, upright leaves. Because there are no branches, its slender profile makes it an ideal floor plant for small spaces. There are also dwarf varieties that form small rosettes of leaves. The most common foliage showcases shades of green with silver-gray horizontal streaks. Some variegated varieties have cream- or gold-colored edges. This plant is extremely drought-tolerant, but its Achilles' heel is too much water. Snake plant needs well-drained potting mix that doesn't hold a lot of water. Ideally, snake plant likes to be in bright but indirect sunlight.
